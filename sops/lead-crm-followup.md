# SOP: Lead Management & CRM Follow-Up

**Owner:** EA / Operations Lead  
**Applies to:** Inbound and outbound lead pipelines, CRM hygiene  
**CRM examples:** HubSpot · Kommo · GoHighLevel · Salesforce

---

## Overview

This SOP ensures every lead is captured, categorized, followed up on, and tracked — with no manual effort required from the executive. The EA owns the full lead lifecycle from entry to handoff.

---

## Lead Intake

### Sources
- Website contact form
- Inbound email
- Social media (LinkedIn DMs, Instagram, etc.)
- Referrals (internal or external)
- Paid campaigns (Apollo.io, ads)

### Step 1 — Log the lead (within 2 hours of receipt)

1. Create or update the lead record in CRM.
2. Fill in mandatory fields:
   - Full name
   - Company / Industry
   - Contact info (email + phone)
   - Source
   - Date of first contact
   - Initial interest / service requested
3. Tag with pipeline stage: `New Lead`
4. Assign to the correct pipeline (e.g., Real Estate · Consulting · Technology)

---

## Follow-Up Cadence

| Day | Action | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 (same day) | Send intro/acknowledgment email | Email |
| Day 2 | Follow-up if no response | Email |
| Day 4 | Phone call attempt + voicemail if no answer | Phone |
| Day 7 | Final follow-up email with value-add (case study, resource) | Email |
| Day 14 | Move to "Nurture" sequence if still no response | CRM automation |

> All touchpoints are logged in CRM with date, channel, and outcome.

---

## CRM Record Standards

Every record must have:

- [ ] Contact info complete and verified
- [ ] Source tagged
- [ ] Last contact date updated after every touchpoint
- [ ] Notes field: plain-language summary of last conversation
- [ ] Next action + due date set
- [ ] Pipeline stage current

**No lead is left without a next action date.**

## Email Templates

### Initial outreach
```
Subject: Following up — [Company/Project Name]

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for reaching out to [Company]. I wanted to follow up and make sure we connect.

[1-sentence value prop relevant to their inquiry]

Would you have 15 minutes this week for a quick call?

Best,
[Name]
```

### Day 7 follow-up
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [First Name],

Just circling back in case this got buried. I'd love to connect when the timing works for you.

In the meantime, here's something that might be relevant: [link or resource]

Happy to work around your schedule.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Pipeline Stage Definitions

| Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| New Lead | First contact made, not yet qualified |
| Contacted | At least one touchpoint completed |
| Qualified | Interest confirmed, budget/timeline discussed |
| Proposal Sent | Formal proposal or quote delivered |
| Negotiation | Terms being discussed |
| Closed Won | Deal signed |
| Closed Lost | No longer pursuing |
| Nurture | Long-term follow-up, not ready now |

---

## Weekly CRM Hygiene Checklist

Every Friday before EOD:

- [ ] All leads contacted this week have updated notes
- [ ] No lead has an overdue next-action date
- [ ] Stale leads (14+ days no response) moved to Nurture
- [ ] Pipeline report pulled and included in Weekly Ops Report
- [ ] Any leads requiring executive input flagged and escalated

---

## Reporting

A CRM summary is included in every **Weekly Ops Report**:

- New leads this week: `X`
- Follow-ups completed: `X`
- Leads advanced in pipeline: `X`
- Deals closed: `X`
- Leads moved to nurture: `X`
