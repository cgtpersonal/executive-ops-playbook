# SOP: Inbox & Calendar Management

**Owner:** EA / Operations Lead  
**Applies to:** Executive inboxes and calendars for multi-venture operators  
**Review cycle:** Quarterly or upon role change

---

## Overview

This SOP governs daily inbox triage, calendar planning, and executive communication. The goal is to ensure the executive has zero administrative friction and full visibility into their day — without having to ask for it.

---

## Daily Rhythm

### 🌅 Morning Block (7:30–9:00 AM)

**Inbox triage — first pass**

1. Open inbox and scan all new messages received since EOD prior day.
2. Apply priority labels:
   - 🔴 **Urgent** — requires same-day response or action
   - 🟡 **Follow-up** — awaiting reply or tracking a thread
   - 🟢 **FYI** — no action needed, archive after reading
   - ⚫ **Delegate/Discard** — forward or delete
3. Draft responses for anything the executive needs to sign off on — leave as draft with a note.
4. Flag any item that requires the executive's personal input for the check-in meeting.

**Check-in meeting (9:00–9:15 AM)**

- Review the day's calendar: confirm all meetings are prepped, briefed, and have materials ready.
- Walk through flagged inbox items — executive makes decisions, I execute.
- Align on the day's #1 priority.

**Deep work block (9:30 AM–12:30 PM)**

- Calendar blocked. No interruptions unless urgent.
- I work on the day's main deliverable + prep materials for upcoming meetings.
- Executive's focus time is also blocked during this window — no meetings scheduled here unless unavoidable.

---

### ☀️ Midday Check (12:30–1:00 PM)

- Second inbox pass: catch anything that came in during the morning.
- Update CRM or task list if any emails generated action items.
- Quick status check on any time-sensitive threads.

---

### 🌆 Afternoon Block (2:00–4:30 PM)

- Execute 3 smaller tasks from the day's list (scheduling, follow-ups, document prep, vendor coordination).
- Respond to non-urgent emails.
- Update any live project docs or trackers.

---

### 🌙 End-of-Day Block (5:00–6:00 PM)

1. Final inbox pass — close out the day's threads.
2. Anything unresolved → moved to tomorrow's priority list with a note on status.
3. Prepare **End-of-Day Briefing** for the executive (see template).
4. Block and confirm tomorrow's calendar.
5. Set reminders for any follow-ups due tomorrow.

---

## Weekly Planning (Sunday Evening)

Every Sunday I prepare the week ahead:

1. Review the executive's calendar for the full week.
2. Set a **two-week calendar horizon**: meetings, blocks, and commitments are placed at least 2 weeks in advance.
3. Block deep work time, travel prep time, and buffer slots.
4. Draft the **Weekly Ops Preview** and share Monday morning before the check-in.

---

## Calendar Principles

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| Deep work blocks are sacred — no meetings | Protects focused output time |
| All meetings have a 10-min prep block before | Executive arrives context-ready |
| 15-min buffer after every 60-min meeting | Recovery + follow-up time |
| External calls blocked in the executive's name | Prevents double-booking |
| Two-week horizon always maintained | No surprises, full control |

---

## Inbox Filters & Folder Structure

```
📁 INBOX
├── 🔴 Action Required (today)
├── 🟡 Awaiting Reply
├── 📋 To Read (FYI, newsletters, reports)
├── 💼 Vendor / Contracts
├── 📅 Meeting-related
└── 🗄️ Archive (processed)
```

Tools used: Gmail filters + Fixer AI for automated triage and follow-up tracking.

---

## Escalation Protocol

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Urgent email arrives during deep work | WhatsApp/Slack ping to executive with 1-line summary |
| Legal or financial document requires signature | Flag immediately, do not wait for EOD |
| Conflict in calendar | Resolve independently if possible; escalate if it involves the executive's preference |
| Unknown sender with high-priority claim | Research sender before forwarding |
